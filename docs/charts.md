# Charts Reference

All charts are generated by `scripts/generate_charts.py` and saved to `charts/` as PNG files at 150 DPI.

---

## 01 — Category Sentiment

**File:** `charts/01_category_sentiment.png`
**Type:** Stacked horizontal bar chart
**Data source:** `feedbacks.csv`

Shows the share of 1-star vs 2–5-star reviews for each sector. Each bar represents 100% of reviews in that category, split by rating tier.

**Key finding:** 86.6% of all platform reviews are 1-star. No sector is dominated by positive reviews.

**README reference:** Finding 1 — The Platform is Dominated by Complaints

## 02 — Top 15 Most Reviewed Companies

## 10 — Sector Risk Matrix

**File:** `charts/10_sector_risk_matrix.png`
**Type:** Scatter plot with quadrant backgrounds and annotated labels
**Data source:** `feedbacks.csv` aggregated by sector

Plots each sector as a bubble at coordinates (total review volume, average rating). Bubble colour encodes average rating via a red-yellow-green colormap. A vertical threshold line at x=200 reviews and a horizontal threshold at y=1.5 divide the chart into four strategic quadrants:

| Quadrant | Label | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Top-left | CONTAINED RISK | Low volume, better rating — watch and protect |
| Top-right | ELEVATED RISK | High volume, better rating — maintain and defend |
| Bottom-left | SERIOUS RISK | Low volume, low rating — emerging risk |
| Bottom-right | CRITICAL ZONE | High volume, low rating — most urgent action needed |

**Key finding:** No sector occupies the top-right (high volume + good rating). All high-volume sectors — Internet Providers, Taxi, Banks, Mobile Operators — sit in the Critical Zone.

**README reference:** Finding 8 — The Sector Risk Matrix Reveals Where Action is Most Urgent

## 11 — Review Activity Stream

**File:** `charts/11_review_stream.png`
**Type:** Time-series area/line chart
**Data source:** `feedbacks.csv` (review_id used as proxy for chronological order)

Shows the density and distribution of review activity from oldest (page 106) to most recent (page 1). Spikes indicate bursts of concentrated review activity.

**Key finding:** Review volume has grown over time. Sudden spikes correspond to service crises or viral complaint moments and serve as an early-warning signal.

**README reference:** Finding 11 — Review Activity Shows Concentration in Certain Periods

## 12 — Top 3 Companies per Category

**File:** `charts/12_top3_per_category.png`
**Type:** Grouped horizontal bar chart
**Data source:** `feedbacks.csv`

For each of the 16 sectors, shows the top 3 most-reviewed companies and their individual review counts. Illustrates how review concentration within each sector shapes public perception.

**Key finding:** In Taxi, Bolt alone represents 81% of all taxi reviews. In Internet Providers, Azeronline accounts for 37%. Category leaders carry disproportionate responsibility for sector-wide reputation.

**README reference:** Finding 12 — Category Leaders Concentrate Public Attention
